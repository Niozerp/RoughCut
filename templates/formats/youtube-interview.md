# Structure Overview

A professional YouTube interview format designed for corporate content creators. Features a strong hook to capture attention, a narrative section with key beats, and a clear call-to-action outro.

## Timing Specifications

### Hook (0:00 - 0:15)
**Duration**: 15 seconds
**Purpose**: Capture attention with strongest soundbite

- Cut to most compelling 15-second segment from interview
- Use upbeat, attention-grabbing music underneath
- Consider adding lower third with speaker name/title

### Narrative (0:15 - 3:45)
**Duration**: 3 minutes 30 seconds
**Purpose**: Main content with 3 key narrative beats

- Cut transcript to exactly 3 strongest narrative beats
- Preserve all original words exactly - never paraphrase
- Match cuts to natural speech pauses
- Background music bed throughout (quieter than hook)
- Add lower third at segment start

### Outro (3:45 - 4:15)
**Duration**: 30 seconds
**Purpose**: Call-to-action and brand conclusion

- Final summary statement or call-to-action
- Music swell for emotional closing
- Standard outro chime/effect
- Total runtime: under 4:15

## Asset Groups

### Music
- **intro_music**: Upbeat attention-grabbing intro music (upbeat, corporate, short, energetic)
- **narrative_bed**: Background music for main content (corporate, bed, neutral, professional)
- **outro_music**: Music swell for emotional closing (corporate, swell, triumphant, inspiring)

### SFX
- **intro_sfx**: Sound effect for hook transition (whoosh, impact, transition)
- **outro_sfx**: Success chime or completion sound (chime, success, corporate)

### VFX
- **lower_third**: Speaker name and title graphic (lower_third, corporate, professional, clean)

## Cutting Rules

- Cut transcript to exactly 3 narrative beats maximum
- Preserve all original words exactly as spoken
- Never paraphrase, summarize, or change speaker's words
- Match cuts to natural speech pauses for smooth flow
- Keep total runtime under 4:15 for optimal YouTube retention
- Select strongest 15-second segment for hook from anywhere in interview
- Ensure narrative beats tell a coherent story in sequence
